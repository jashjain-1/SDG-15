Why SDG 15 Matters

SDG 15 focuses on conserving forests, combating land degradation, and protecting biodiversity. Forests are not just collections of trees; they are the lungs of the Earth and home to nearly 80% of terrestrial species. Today, deforestation continues at an alarming rate due to agriculture, urban expansion, and illegal logging.

Over 10 million hectares of forest are lost every year. This drastically affects rainfall patterns, increases carbon emissions, disrupts ecosystems, and accelerates climate change. Forest conservation is no longer optional; it is an emergency priority for all nations.

The mission of SDG 15 is to restore degraded land, conserve endangered species, strengthen forest governance, and promote sustainable ecosystem-based solutions for future generations.
